## 📊 What the sector's naming tells us

Big names in the space:
- **Crypto / sportsbook:** Stake, Rollbit, Roobet, Duelbits, BC.Game, Bovada, Sportsbet.io
- **Social / sweepstakes:** Stake.us, Chumba, Pulsz, McLuck, WOW Vegas, LuckyLand, High 5, Crown Coins

**Patterns:**
1. **Short and punchy** — one or two syllables (Stake, Roo, Chumba, Pulsz).
2. **Betting-word + suffix** — `-bet`, `-bit`, `-bits` (Roobet, Rollbit, Duelbits).
3. **Luck / fortune themes** — McLuck, LuckyLand, Pulsz.
4. **Made-up but pronounceable** — Chumba, Roobet, Pulsz (easier to trademark + get the `.com`).
5. **Coins / points framing** for social ones — "Coins," "Crown Coins" — because they're *not* real money.

**Strategic angle for us:** since this is **points, not money**, leaning into
**chips / stacks / tokens / credit** imagery is both on-brand *and* legally cleaner —
it sidesteps real-money language entirely, fitting the no-KYC, closed-loop identity.

**Betting slang worth mining:** *action* (having a bet on), *edge*, *marker* (casino
credit), *sharp* (smart bettor), *lock* (sure thing), *chalk* (the favorite), *stack*
(pile of chips), *ante*, *parlay*, *streak*, *whale / high roller*, *degen*.

## 💡 Name ideas by direction

### A. Points-native (chips / stacks — fits "no real money")
- **Stack** / **Stacks** / **StackHouse** — a stack of chips; "stacking wins." Clean, modern, poker-rooted.
- **Chip** / **Chips** — literally the points. Simple, casino-native, friendly.
- **Marker** — casino term for *credit extended to a player* → ties directly to the credit/balance `core`. Sophisticated, uncommon.
- **Tally** / **Pip** — the running count; "pip" is the smallest unit (clean, minimalist).

### B. Sportsbook-leaning / smart-bettor
- **Sharp** / **GoSharp** — a sharp is a winning bettor. Confident, sleek.
- **Edge** / **TrueEdge** — your advantage; also nods to "house edge" transparency.
- **Lock** / **LockIn** — a sure bet. Punchy, memorable.
- **Chalk** — the favorite. Short, insider, clean.
- **Parlay** — instantly says "sportsbook," fun to say.

### C. Clean / premium minimalist (fits "the interface is the product")
- **Ante** / **AnteUp** — universal, short, easy.
- **Pulse** — live odds pulsing in real time. ⚠️ *Pulsz* exists — maybe too close.
- **Apex** / **Vertex** / **Onyx** / **Slate** — neutral, premium, abstract.
- **Crown** — winner energy; common in the sweepstakes space (Crown Coins).

### D. Edgy / playful (keeps the DimeBag energy)
- **Degen** / **DegenHouse** — "degenerate gambler" is *the* in-culture term in crypto casinos. High-risk, high-reward brand.
- **Dime** / **OnaDime** / **DimeClub** — keeps the heritage ("a dime" = a $1,000 bet); "turn on a dime" = fast.
- **Tilt** — emotional over-betting. ⚠️ *Full Tilt Poker* association — check trademark.
- **Bag** / **Bagged** / **SecureTheBag** — "the bag" = winnings; very current slang.

### E. Luck / fortune
- **Fortuna** / **Fortune** — the goddess of luck; classy.
- **Mojo** / **Fluke** / **Lucky** — playful, light.

## ⭐ Top 5 picks (with reasoning)

| Name | Why it works | Watch-outs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Stack** | Points-native (chips, not cash), clean, works for casino *and* sportsbook, modern. | `stack.com` almost certainly taken — likely needs a modifier (PlayStack, StackHQ) or a `.gg` / `.io`. |
| **Marker** | Genuinely clever — it's casino credit, mapping exactly to the credit/balance core. Premium, distinctive. | Less "fun"; slightly insider (some won't get the reference). |
| **Sharp** | Confident, sleek, bridges both products, easy to brand around (clean blue/steel palette writes itself). | Common word → domain/trademark crowded. |
| **Ante** | Short, universal, friendly, easy to say and spell. Great for a clean minimalist brand. | Generic-ish; trademark check needed. |
| **Degen** | If we want to *own* the edgy DimeBag spirit — huge cultural resonance in crypto/casino. | Polarizing; not "premium." Strong identity, narrower audience. |

## ⚠️ Before committing to any name

Each candidate needs three checks:
1. **Domain availability** — `.com` / `.gg` / `.io`
2. **Trademark** — USPTO + EU
3. **App-store name collision**

Common words (Stack, Edge, Sharp) will be the hardest to clear; coined / compound
names (Roobet-style) are easiest.
